Collin Mcrae

"When in doubt flat out"

Timeline

  • 1968 - Born in Lanark, Scotland, into a motorsport family; his father, Jimmy McRae, was a five-time British Rally Champion, inspiring his passion for rally racing.
  • 1986 - Begins his rally career in local events, showcasing his aggressive driving style and raw talent behind the wheel.
  • 1987 - Wins the Scottish Rally Championship, proving himself as a rising star in the rallying world at just 19 years old.
  • 1991 - Joins the Subaru World Rally Team and wins the British Rally Championship, cementing his reputation as a future World Rally Championship (WRC) contender.
  • 1993 - Earns his first WRC victory at Rally New Zealand, making history as the first British driver to win a WRC event for Subaru.
  • 1995 - Becomes the first British driver to win the World Rally Championship, driving the iconic Subaru Impreza 555, securing his place as a motorsport legend.
  • 1999 - Moves to Ford and helps develop the Ford Focus WRC, achieving multiple rally wins and proving his adaptability across different manufacturers.
